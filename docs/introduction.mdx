The Mint is a behavioral research project studying the financial habits of young people. Over 12 months, a group of volunteer participants records every financial transaction — income, expenses, transfers — through a structured tracking tool built specifically for this study.

This is not a budgeting app. This is a laboratory.

---

## Why this study?

Most financial tools tell you what to do with your money. The Mint asks a different question first:

> Does knowing in advance what a financial decision will cost actually change the way we use our money?

We believe that financial behavior is not a knowledge problem — it's a lucidity problem. Before building tools that prescribe, we want to understand how our generation actually behaves.

---

## What we are building

The data collected through this study will serve three specific objectives:

1. **A dataset** — pseudo anonymized, open data on the financial habits of young people in Benin
2. **An application** — a personal finance tracking tool built from real behavioral insights
3. **A decision-making tool** — to help individuals anticipate the cost of their financial decisions before making them

---

## How it works

Each participant receives a personal workspace — a structured Google Sheet connected to a data entry form. Every financial transaction is recorded in real time: amount, category, payment method, budget, and more.

The data is pseudo anonymized from the start. At the end of the study, it is fully anonymized before any publication.
